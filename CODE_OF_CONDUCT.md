# Code of Conduct

LoopPilot is a research and engineering project about controlled AI automation. The community norm is simple: be precise, respectful, safety-conscious, and evidence-oriented.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and direct.
- Critique claims, designs, tests, and evidence without attacking people.
- Respect privacy, unpublished work, credentials, and local user data.
- State uncertainty when evidence is incomplete.
- Prefer reproducible commands and artifacts over vague assertions.
- Preserve safety gates and human review boundaries.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Publishing private credentials, unpublished papers, private repositories, or personal data.
- Pressuring maintainers to bypass SafetyGate, review gates, or secret-handling rules.
- Misrepresenting test results, paper evidence, or artifact status.
- Introducing hidden side effects in setup, tests, examples, or demos.

## Reporting

For now, report conduct or safety concerns to the maintainer listed in `GOVERNANCE.md`. Include enough context to reproduce the issue where possible, but do not include secrets or private data.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content, close issues or pull requests, block unsafe changes, or pause collaboration when behavior threatens people, privacy, or project safety.
